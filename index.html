Neon Genesis Evangelion, also known simply as Evangelion or Eva, is a Japanese mecha anime television series produced by Gainax and animated by Tatsunoko, directed by Hideaki Anno and broadcast on TV Tokyo from October 1995 to March 1996. Evangelion is set fifteen years after a worldwide cataclysm named Second Impact, particularly in the futuristic fortified city of Tokyo-3.
The protagonist is Shinji Ikari, a teenage boy who is recruited by his father Gendo to the shadowy organization Nerv to pilot a giant bio-machine mecha named Evangelion into combat against beings known as Angels.

nerv

'God's in his heaven'

NERV is an international organization with their center of operation located in the city of Tokyo-3, Japan. More specifically, they run the majority of their research and operations out of NERV Headquarters, a large facility located in the GeoFront. Outside of their main operation in Japan, NERV also has operations in the United States, Germany and possibly more. As far as the world is concerned, NERV is under the control of the United Nations however the truth isn't so simple. In reality, the shadowy organization SEELE are in control of the United Nations and as such, they are also direct overseers to NERV.
Despite this oversight, they still mostly operate as an independent organization with SEELE being their main source of funding.

evas

(!Mechas)

Evangelion are giant, living humanoid beings of near-godlike power and are the namesake of the Evangelion franchise. They are present in nearly every single franchise entry, although their functionality and role can vary greatly depending on the story being told. Originally created by Gehirn and further developed by NERV under the supervision of SEELE, the Evangelion are born from samples of the first Angel, Adam, with the exception of Evangelion Unit-01, which was created from the second angel, Lilith. While the Evangelion were supposedly created to combat the Angel threat, their most important functions, and one of their greatest secrets, is to initiate Third Impact.

angels

Those who ate fruit of life

Angels are the central antagonistic force in Neon Genesis Evangelion series. They are giant malevolent monsters of extremely varied shapes and powers, originating from a being known as Adam. Their sole goal is to reach Lilith, a giant humanoid lifeform restrained in Terminal Dogma of NERV Organization's headquarters, in order to cause Third Impact, an apocalyptic event that would annihilate all life on Earth within the blink of an eye.

second impact

'The story presented to the public was just a cover-up'

Second Impact is a global cataclysm which occurred on September 13th, 2000, when a massive explosion occurred in Antarctica which melted the polar cap and even altered Earth's axis. The explosion resulted in massive tsunamis, followed by a dramatic increase in global sea levels which flooded most coastal cities, and caused a catastrophic shift in global weather patterns. According to the official report on the disaster by the United Nations, the massive explosion was the result of a meteorite impact that struck Mount Markham. Hence the event was dubbed 'Second Impact'.
Known only to a select few was that the meteorite impact story was simply a cover up: the true cause of Second Impact was when the First Angel, Adam, exploded as the result of a 'Contact Experiment' carried out by the Katsuragi Expedition, which was itself secretly backed by the mysterious Seele organization.

philosophy

'The closer we become, the more deeply we hurt each other'

The characters of Neon Genesis Evangelion all feel lost and alienated because of a lack of strong connections to parent figures, or peers. They have experienced pain, and losses, that make them wary of growing close to others, and they employ defensive mechanisms to try and protect themselves from further pain.
This is an example of the Hedgehog`s Dilemma, coming from the writings of Arthur Schopenhauer, and forming the title of the fourth ep the series. Schopenhauer tells the parable of porcupines, coming together for warmth but driven apart again by the pain of each other`s spines, mirroring the human condition of interpersonal distance driven by the desire to avoid harm.
Though we crave connection, we are unable to ever fully satisfy these desires.

the end

You are (not) alone

Evangelion is not just a typical mecha anime. It delves deep into the complex psychology of its characters, exploring themes of identity, existentialism, and the nature of human relationships. The series masterfully combines intense action sequences with thought-provoking storytelling, creating a truly unique and unforgettable experience. What sets Evangelion apart is its exploration of the human psyche, as it delves into the fears, anxieties, and traumas of its characters. It challenges conventional notions of heroism and confronts the existential questions that arise in the face of overwhelming adversity. Evangelion has had a lasting impact on the anime industry and pop culture as a whole.
Its intricate plot, rich symbolism, and emotional depth have made it a favorite among fans and critics alike. Whether you're a fan of mecha anime or someone looking for a thought-provoking and visually stunning series, Evangelion is a must-watch that will leave a impression.
